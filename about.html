Our Mission

Exceptional cashmere and merino, crafted to last.

No compromise on quality. Every Wolly Lully piece starts with full material specifications — composition, ply count, micron grade, and weight — published openly on every product page. You know exactly what you're buying before you add to cart.

We believe the cashmere market has been opaque for too long. "Luxury" is a word that gets used to justify vague claims and inflated prices. We prefer numbers: <19 micron. 280 gsm. 2-ply. Those are the things that determine how a sweater feels in five years, not marketing copy.

Our Materials

What we use and why.

Grade A Cashmere — 100% 2-ply, <19 micron
Cashmere is graded by fibre fineness (measured in microns). Below 19 micron is the industry threshold for the finest grade — fibres this fine sit below skin's irritation threshold, meaning no scratchiness even for sensitive skin. Our crewnecks and accessories use this grade exclusively.

2-ply means the yarn is twisted from two strands rather than one. The result: a fabric that holds its shape better over time, pills less in the first few wears, and has a slightly more structured hand feel than single-ply. It's not heavier — it's more durable.

Merino-Cashmere Blend — 80% merino, 20% cashmere, 240 gsm
The blend is not a compromise — it's a deliberate choice. Merino wool has natural temperature-regulating properties that pure cashmere doesn't match: it warms when cool and breathes when active. Adding 20% cashmere introduces the softness and drape that makes merino feel exceptional rather than merely functional.

The practical upside: merino blends are machine washable on a wool cycle. For customers who want cashmere-level softness in a sweater they can wear daily and launder easily, the 80/20 blend is the right answer. Priced accordingly at CHF 195 versus CHF 285 for pure cashmere — the difference reflects the material cost, not a quality compromise.

What we don't claim
We do not describe our products as "sustainable," "ethical," or "responsible." These terms are imprecise in the textile industry and frequently unverifiable. We focus on what we can measure and prove: fibre grade, weight, construction, and price. If you want to understand what you're buying, our product specifications tell you more than any certification would.
